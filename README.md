# Oceancode
This is a two-player game. Player 1 is Fish Red, and Player 2 is Fish Blue. The round lasts for one minute. At the start of the game, one player is randomly selected to be the tagger. The tagger’s goal is to tag the other fish before the timer runs out. If the tagger successfully tags the other fish, the roles immediately switch. The fish who was tagged becomes the new tagger and must try to tag the other fish. Players will continue switching roles each time a tag occurs until the timer ends.There are important environmental obstacles in the game. The spikes represent plastic pollution in the ocean. If the fish who is not the tagger runs into the spikes (plastic), that fish automatically becomes the tagger. The other fish does not become the tagger in this situation.There is also a green platform in the game that represents ocean plants, such as seaweed. The fish can swim onto the green platform as part of the environment while trying to avoid being tagged.When the one-minute timer runs out, the fish who is currently the tagger loses the round. A results screen will then appear, showing the winner.
